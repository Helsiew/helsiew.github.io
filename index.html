Harry's International · /001/

Marketing, Brand Repositioning & New Menu Launches

Email MarketingPaid AdsDigital CampaignsBrand Strategy
The Challenge

Harry's had been a Singapore fixture for 30 years, but familiarity had become its biggest obstacle. The brand was remembered, not chosen. Customers saw it as a place to drink, not a destination to dine. With 17 outlets to revitalise, the brief was clear: reposition Harry's as a go-to restaurant for all occasions, not just an afterthought on a night out.

My Role

As the sole marketer, I owned the full marketing function end-to-end: campaign planning and execution, always-on and ad-hoc paid media (Meta and Google Ads), website maintenance and microsite builds, monthly EDM copywriting and design, social media content direction and community management, partnership management, brand compliance, and reporting across all marketing and reservations platforms.

The Strategy

Repositioning required change from the inside out. Internally, we relaunched the menu around local dishes with a western twist, refreshed outlet interiors and exteriors, and rebuilt the loyalty programme with compelling member benefits and exclusive promotions. Externally, the strategy centred on giving both lapsed and new customers a compelling reason to walk back in, stay for the food, and keep coming back through rewards.

The Execution

New menu launches were activated with 1-for-1 promotional mechanics to drive trial and footfall. Thematic festive campaigns reinforced brand presence at key calendar moments. Member-exclusive mini campaigns deepened loyalty and rewarded existing sign-ups. Across all touchpoints, the brand's digital look and feel was refreshed while staying true to Harry's voice, mission, and values.

Harry's International · /002/

Loyalty Programme Refresh — Win a Business Class Trip to Koh Samui

CRM & LoyaltyEmail MarketingPaid AdsSponsorships
The Challenge

Harry's had a loyalty programme, but it had never been properly campaigned. With a refreshed web-app in the works, the opportunity was to do more than announce a new look — to drive meaningful sign-ups and shift customer spending behaviour. The objectives were clear: grow the member base and encourage a minimum spend of $100 per customer.

My Role

I led the campaign end-to-end alongside the CRM team — briefing and managing an external creative agency on messaging and positioning, sourcing and managing nano KOLs, building the campaign microsite, producing content and EDMs, running digital ads, and managing the campaign sponsorship.

The Strategy

The mechanic had to be simple enough to participate in, but with a prize compelling enough to drive action. Sign up, unlock membership bonuses immediately, then spend $100 for a chance to win a fully paid business-class trip for two to a Koh Samui resort retreat. The prize gave people a genuine reason to sign up and return, while the instant membership perks rewarded them from day one.

The Execution

Beyond owned media, nano KOLs were brought in to extend reach organically — because nothing sells a good deal like a trusted recommendation in your feed. In-store, we ran a parallel internal contest across all 17 outlets, motivating outlet teams to drive sign-ups on the ground. Marketing doesn't stop at the customer-facing layer; getting the frontline team behind the campaign was just as important as the digital push.

Publicis · Singapore Police Force · /003/

Anti-Scam Awareness Campaign 2025

Content StrategyCreator PartnershipsPublic EducationGovernment
🥇 GOLD — MEA Excellence in Content Marketing 2025
The Challenge

Scam cases in Singapore were on the rise, and the tactics were evolving faster than public awareness could keep up. By 2024, Singapore had recorded tens of thousands of scam cases annually, with losses running into the hundreds of millions. The challenge wasn't just awareness — it was relevance. Scams span five distinct categories: investment scams, love scams, fake friend scams, government impersonation, and phishing. Each preys on different vulnerabilities, different demographics, and different emotional triggers. A one-size-fits-all approach wouldn't cut it.

My Role

I led content strategy, campaign planning, stakeholder management, cross-functional coordination, and execution with content aggregators and creators — from brief to final delivery.

The Strategy

We started with the human truth: people don't get scammed because they're careless, they get scammed because scammers are sophisticated. The strategy was to meet different consumer groups where they were most vulnerable — breaking audiences down by lifestyle, emotional triggers, and platform behaviour to determine what kind of content would actually land.

The Execution

Rather than generic warnings, we built a content ecosystem that brought scam scenarios to life: dramatised situations showing the exact moment a person becomes vulnerable, explainers on how to spot a scam, real-life victim stories, behind-the-scenes looks at scam syndicates, and coverage of what authorities are actively doing to fight back. We also encouraged ongoing participation through SPF's ScamShield initiative, educating the public on the scam call hotline and the importance of reporting near-misses. A separate content strand targeted overseas audiences on Xiaohongshu, addressing job scams targeting individuals seeking employment in Singapore.

Publicis · SCDF · /004/

"What's Your Emergency?" Behaviour Change Campaign (2023–2024)

Content StrategyBehaviour ChangeTikTokGovernment
🏆
TikTok Best Branding 2024
🥈
MEA Brand Awareness Silver 2024
🥉
Markies Gov/Non-Profit Bronze 2023
The Challenge

Total EMS calls to SCDF surged to 256,837 in 2022, a 20.2% increase from 213,615 in 2021. Non-emergency calls grew by 27.5% and false alarms by 8.8%, tying up resources that should be reserved for life-threatening situations. The root behaviour was deeply human: when something goes wrong, the instinct is to call for help immediately. But not everything is an emergency. The challenge was to shift that instinct through education, without diminishing urgency when it truly matters.

My Role

I built the content strategy alongside the team, managed stakeholder and external partner relationships, oversaw budget management, and led cross-functional coordination across the campaign.

The Strategy

The core message was simple: Call 995 — but only when it's a real emergency. Always-on, bite-sized content was deployed across multiple formats, each playing a distinct role. A music video anchored the campaign for top-of-mind recall. Short branded ads educated audiences on the three most common non-emergency situations. Content creators across different categories ensured the message stayed relevant and engaging across audience segments. During festive seasons, KOLs were activated to ride on trending moments and deliver timely education on what does and doesn't constitute an emergency.

The Execution

Three content pillars worked in tandem: the anchor music video for awareness and recall; short branded ads showcasing real non-emergency scenarios; and a creator network providing always-on reach across lifestyle, family, and community categories. Non-conveyance messaging was woven throughout, directing non-emergency cases to visit the nearest clinic or call 1777 instead.

SG Farm Tycoon — Urban Farming Education on Roblox

GamificationContent StrategyYouth EngagementGovernment
🥇 GOLD — Best Use of Gamification · Marketing Events Awards Singapore 2024
The Brief

Singapore's food security depends on growing local — but getting Gen Z and Gen Alpha to care about urban farming required meeting them somewhere they already were. The Singapore Food Agency needed an approach that went beyond conventional education to make the message genuinely stick with a generation that expects to be entertained first.

The Strategy

If the audience lives on Roblox, take the campaign to Roblox. SG Farm Tycoon was designed as an immersive farming game that wove educational content about modern agriculture, urban farming technologies, and Singapore's food future directly into gameplay. SFA's local produce mascots — Caixinderella, Eggs Benedette C, and Seabasstian Water — gave the experience a familiar, playful identity that resonated with younger players.

The Execution

The game balanced captivating gameplay with layered learning across varied levels and challenges, letting players explore real farming concepts without it feeling like a lesson. The result was an experience young audiences chose to engage with on their own terms, for longer than expected.

Reprise · UEM Sunrise · /006/

The Happy Chase — Property Gameshow Campaign

Campaign StrategyGamificationLead GenerationSocial Media
The Challenge

COVID-19 didn't just pause daily life — it froze life decisions. Purchasing a home requires confidence in the future, and in 2020, that confidence had all but evaporated. Showgalleries were closed, families were separated, and the idea of committing to a property felt impossible. For UEM Sunrise, the pipeline of prospective buyers had stalled entirely.

The Strategy

Instead of waiting for the world to reopen, we brought the show gallery to people's living rooms. The insight was simple: Malaysians were home, they were online, and live content was having a moment. We created The Happy Chase, a gamified live property gameshow on Facebook that let viewers explore homes, participate in quizzes, and engage with the brand in real time. Crucially, every episode was fully live, not pre-recorded, which made it feel like an event worth showing up for.

My Role

I helped plan and execute the campaign from digital and social content, arranging scripts, and adding a gamified quiz for viewers to participate in between online show home walkthroughs. I engaged Baki Zainal as host, alongside a representative from UEM Sunrise.

The Execution

Four consecutive live episodes were produced, each featuring virtual show home walkthroughs interspersed with interactive quiz segments to keep viewers engaged. TV host Baki Zainal fronted the show alongside a UEM Sunrise representative, bringing energy and credibility to what could easily have felt like a glorified sales presentation.

Publicis · Building & Construction Authority · /009/

Reframing the Built Environment for Young Talent

Content StrategyEmployer BrandingVideoSocial Media
The Challenge

Singapore's Built Environment sector was modernising fast — drones, automation, robotics, green buildings — but its reputation hadn't caught up. Among students and young graduates, the industry was still seen as physically demanding, low-tech, and unglamorous. BCA needed to change that perception to attract the next generation of talent before the pipeline ran dry.

The Strategy

Rather than telling young people the industry had changed, we showed them. A documentary-style content approach put real projects and real people front and centre — high-tech infrastructure like Sensoryscape and the Deep Tunnel Sewerage System Phase 2, and women carving out careers in construction engineering. Stories that challenged assumptions and made the sector feel like somewhere a young, ambitious person would actually want to build a future.

The Execution

Content was distributed across YouTube, Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok to meet students and graduating cohorts where they spent their time. Each platform played to its strengths — longer documentary formats for YouTube, snackable cuts for TikTok and Instagram Reels.

Publicis · Land Transport Authority · /010/

People Behind the Tracks

Content StrategyEmployer BrandingDocumentary SeriesGovernment
The Challenge

Singapore's public transport network runs with remarkable precision — but the people who make that possible are largely invisible to the commuters who rely on them daily. LTA needed to shift that, building both public appreciation for the workforce and a stronger employer brand to attract new talent into the industry.

The Strategy

Institutional messaging about transport infrastructure doesn't move people. Personal stories do. The approach was to go human-first — a documentary content series that put the spotlight on the real individuals keeping Singapore's trains and buses running, in partnership with SMRT, SBS Transit, and other operators.

The Execution

A three-part video series brought different roles and stories to life, turning what could have been a corporate communications exercise into something people actually wanted to watch. By making the workforce the heroes of the story, the content served a dual purpose: deepening public appreciation for the industry and repositioning it as a meaningful place to build a career.

Jirnexu / RinggitPlus · /011/

Malaysian Financial Literacy Survey 2022

Brand PlanningResearchGo-to-MarketFinancial Services
The Brief

The RinggitPlus Malaysian Financial Literacy Survey is an annual national study examining how Malaysians save, spend, plan for retirement, adopt insurance, and navigate digital finance — broken down across income levels to surface actionable insights for the industry and policymakers. Each year, the study sharpens its focus on a specific behaviour or gap in financial literacy, ensuring the findings stay timely and relevant.

My Role

I owned the study end-to-end: from research strategy and survey design through to go-to-market execution, sponsorship development, and stakeholder management. Findings were presented at a media launch event attended by publications and sponsors, positioning RinggitPlus as the definitive voice on Malaysian personal finance behaviour and driving brand visibility across the financial services landscape.

The Impact

The annual survey established RinggitPlus as a credible, go-to authority on Malaysian financial literacy, generating consistent media coverage and reinforcing the brand's relevance with both consumers and industry partners.
